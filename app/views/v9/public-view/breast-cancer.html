Adult screening programme Breast cancer

In the UK, breast cancer is the most common type of cancer among women. Approximately 45,000 cases of breast cancer are diagnosed every year, usually in women who are over 50 years of age and who have reached menopause. However, it is possible for women of any age to be affected by breast cancer and, in rare cases, the condition can also affect men.

UK NSC's screening recommendation The last UK NSC review of this condition occurred in April 2019.

Screening is currently recommended for this condition

Following a review of the evidence against strict criteria, the UK NSC currently recommends introducing a national screening programme for carrier or new-born screening for spinal muscular atrophy.

Key findings supporting the UK NSC recommendation from 2018:

  • there was is evidence about how acceptable the screening programme is
  • there is evidence on how to support individuals who need to make difficult decisions following carrier screening
