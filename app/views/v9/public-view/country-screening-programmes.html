Overview

Each UK country sets its own screening policy based on the recommendations of the UK National Screening Committee (UK NSC). This means that there are some differences in the screening offered.

Screening in Wales

In Wales, ministers set screening policy based on the advice of the UK NSC and the Wales Screening Committee.

See a list of the current screening programmes in Wales.

Screening in Scotland

In Scotland, ministers set screening policy based on the advice from the UK NSC and the Scottish Screening Committee.

See a list of the current screening programmes in Scotland.

Screening in Northern Ireland

In Northern Ireland, the health minister sets screening policy based on the advice of the UK NSC and the Northern Ireland Screening Committee.

See a list of the current screening programmes in Northern Ireland.

Screening in England

In England, ministers set screening policy based on the advice of the UK NSC.

See a list of the current screening programmes in England.
