Plain English summary

Key findings

  • the most common tests for alcohol misuse are questionnaires which are unsuitable when used within a whole population screening programme.
  • different people can safely drink different amounts of alcohol, depending on factors such as their age, sex and ethnicity. For a screening test to be reliable it will have to consider these factors by defining test ‘cut-off levels’.
  • the review did not find any research that showed a whole population screening programme would help to reduce the harms from alcohol misuse in the long term

Read more about the evidence for alcohol misuse through the evidence review]

More about alcohol misuse

Drinking a moderate amount of alcohol will not do you any physical or psychological harm. However, for some people, social drinking can lead to heavier drinking, which can cause serious health problems.

Read more about alcohol misuse on NHS Choices
